## 🔧 APPROVED WITH REVISIONS (Changes Required Before Launch)

### Script 1: "The Blind Spot" — APPROVED WITH REVISED CLOSE

**Issue:** The close — *"I've been using these for 30 nights. My morning face looks different. My jaw looks different. And I changed literally nothing else."* — is vague, unsubstantiated, and generic. Every before/after supplement ad uses this structure. It will not convert the skeptical looksmaxxer who has been burned by overpromised products before.

**REVISED CLOSE (Replace lines from 25 seconds onward):**

> *"I've been using these for 30 nights. Here's what actually changed: I stopped waking up with a dry mouth. My jaw stopped feeling loose in the morning. And when I looked at my morning face at day 30 versus day one — the puffiness was gone. Not dramatically. But consistently. Every single morning."*

**[ON SCREEN TEXT — revised]:** `30 NIGHTS. CONSISTENT RESULTS. LINK IN BIO.`

> *"They have a 30-night money-back guarantee. So the only thing you're risking is 30 nights of actually sleeping with your mouth closed. Start tonight."*

**Why this is stronger:** It replaces vague aesthetic claims with specific, sensory, believable details (dry mouth, jaw looseness, puffiness). It sets realistic expectations, which builds trust. The "not dramatically, but consistently" line is counterintuitively powerful — it sounds honest, which makes the whole testimonial more credible.

**Hook recommendation for Script 1:** Launch Hook A ("You've been mewing wrong for months") as primary. It's the most confrontational and will generate the highest watch-through rate for the mewing-aware audience. Hook C ("POV: You've been mewing for a year and your face looks exactly the same") as secondary — it's more empathetic and will perform better with a slightly older or more skeptical segment.

### Script 2: "Morning Face" — APPROVED WITH REVISED CLOSE AND VISUAL GUARDRAIL

**Issue 1:** The close — *"This is what 30 nights looks like. Same person. Same lighting."* — sets up a before/after comparison that, if the visual improvement is subtle or inconsistent across creators, will look like a failed promise. It also edges toward implied medical/cosmetic claims territory.

**REVISED CLOSE:**

> *"This is what 30 nights of nasal breathing looks like on my face. I didn't change my diet. I didn't change my skincare. I just stopped mouth breathing for eight hours every night. The puffiness that I thought was just my face — it was a symptom. And this is what happens when you fix the cause."*

**[ON SCREEN TEXT — revised]:** `FIXED THE CAUSE. NOT THE SYMPTOM.`

> *"Link in bio. Thirty-day money-back guarantee. If your mornings don't feel different, they'll refund you. No questions."*

**Why this is stronger:** "Fixed the cause, not the symptom" is a positioning line that elevates the product above cosmetic gimmicks. It also shifts the claim from visual transformation (hard to prove, legally risky) to functional improvement (more defensible). The puffiness-as-symptom framing is medically adjacent but not a medical claim.

**Visual Guardrail for Before/After:** The before/after visual in this script MUST be shot with identical lighting, identical camera distance, and identical time of day. If the production team cannot guarantee this, remove the side-by-side comparison and replace with a single "morning face, day 30" shot with the creator speaking to camera. A bad before/after is worse than no before/after.

**Hook recommendation for Script 2:** Launch Hook A ("Why does my face look like THIS every morning?") as primary. The raw morning face close-up is the strongest visual hook in the package. Hook D ("Nobody talks about what mouth breathing does to your face while you sleep") as secondary — the conspiratorial whisper format performs well with the aesthetics-aware audience.

### Static Ad 3: "The Science Card" — APPROVED WITH REVISED DATA CLAIMS

**Issue:** The "+11 avg HRV in 30 days (self-reported)" claim is a liability. Self-reported data without a sample size, methodology, or third-party verification is an FTC red flag, especially for health-adjacent products. The claim also sets a specific numerical expectation that individual users may not meet, creating refund pressure and negative reviews.

**REVISED DATA CALLOUTS:**

Replace the AERYS USERS box with:

```
┌─────────────────────┐
│    AERYS PROTOCOL   │
│  Reported by users: │
│  ↓ Morning puffiness│
│  ↑ Morning energy   │
│  ↓ Dry mouth on wake│
│  "Woke up sharper"  │
└─────────────────────┘
```

**REVISED SOCIAL PROOF LINE:**

Replace: *"My HRV went up 11 points in the first month. Changed nothing else." — Marcus D., Austin TX*

With: *"I've tracked my sleep for three years. Adding AERYS was the first change that actually showed up in how I felt in the morning, not just on an app." — Marcus D., Austin TX*

**Why this is stronger:** The revised testimonial is more credible because it's experiential rather than numerical. It also speaks directly to the biohacker audience's frustration with data that doesn't translate to real-world results — a known pain point in the optimization community.

**Retain the nasal breathing vs. mouth breathing comparison boxes.** The nitric oxide / cortisol / deep sleep data is well-supported in the literature and is not a product claim — it's a mechanism explanation. This is legally safer and scientifically defensible.

## 2) TikTok Spark Ad Authorization Workflow

1. Creator publishes final approved post organically.
2. Creator opens post settings and enables ad authorization.
3. Creator generates Spark code and shares it to AERYS media buyer.
4. Media buyer confirms post ID, code expiry date, and ad account linkage.
5. Compliance check before spend activation (claims, captions, subtitles, on-screen text).
6. Launch in test ad groups by angle (do not mix all hooks in one ad group).

**Operational note:** Any post without valid Spark authorization code is ineligible for scale and should not enter primary testing.

## 3) Landing Page Alignment Brief (Critical for CVR)

If the ad angle is jawline/mewing, the destination page must mirror that promise in the first screen.

**Above-the-fold requirements:**
- Headline aligned to "nighttime breathing = daytime facial progress"
- Product visual + creator-style proof image (non-medical, realistic)
- 3 fast bullets: less morning puffiness, less dry mouth, more consistent mornings
- Price clarity ($34) + 30-night money-back guarantee visible without scrolling
- Clear CTA button above the fold

**Trust/objection handling block:**
- "Does this replace mewing?" → No, it supports nighttime consistency.
- "Will this force a dramatic change?" → No overpromises; outcome is consistency-first.
- "What if it doesn't work for me?" → 30-night guarantee, straightforward refund.

---

## 4) Compliance Guardrails

- Never claim diagnosis, treatment, cure, or guaranteed facial restructuring.
- Avoid hard numbers unless substantiated by validated methodology.
- Prefer experience-based outcomes ("felt sharper," "less dry mouth") over biomarker promises.
- Before/after assets must be controlled or replaced with single-shot testimonial format.
